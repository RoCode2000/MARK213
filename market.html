PESTEL Analysis

Scope: Interdental brush market in Singapore

Political

  • In 1969, there was an emphasis on teaching youths dental hygiene as part of their school curriculum.
  • Later in 2001, the first Smile Contest for senior citizens took place to promote dental hygiene.
  • Oral Health month was introduced in 2004, providing free dental checkups at 320 participating clinis throughout the month.
  • The Health Promotion Board announced that it would offer free oral health screening for preschoolers to at least 1100 childcare centres by 2020.

The Singapore government's focus on dental hygiene will help the oral care market in Singapore as more Singaporeans are taught the important of oral hygiene.

Economical

  • Singapore's GDP growth is projected to come in at around 1.0% in 2023 and 1.0 to 3.0% in 2024.
  • "For a developed economy, an annual GDP growth rate of 2-3% is considered normal."
  • Singapore's unemployment rate in 2023 was 1.9%.
  • "A reasonable unemployment rate is somewhere between 3-5%. When unemployment is low, workers are usually paid more and will spend more, which is good for the economy."

Due to Singapore's stable economy and relatively low unemployment rate, workers in Singapore will be more willing to spend.

  • Muslims are standing with Palestine, spreading news of Palestine throughout social media.

This will affect Oral B's sales in Singapore as they are part of P&G.

  • The revenue of the E-commerce market in Singapore was forecast to increase between 2024 and 2028 by 2.6 billion U.S. dollars.

Environmental

  • "Nearly 9 out of 10 shoppers in Singapore are willing to pay a premium for sustainable goods".
  • Interdental brushes server the same purpose as dental picks/floss.
  • Unlike dental picks/floss, interdental brushes are reusable.
  • Interdental brushes may be reused as long as the bristles do not look worn or shabby.

Singaporeans may be more likely to choose interdental brushes as they are more sustainable for the environment.

Legal

  • Health Sciences Authority has defined Oral B products as a cosmetic product, subjecting it to the Health Products Act 2007.
  • This legislation has various restrictions regarding the contents that make up the product, advertising and representation of the product.
  • This includes labeling and packaging of the product which must not misrepresent the function or efficacy of the product.

These stringent regulations pose as an additional barrier that adversely impacts the way Oral B products are formulated and advertised to the public.
