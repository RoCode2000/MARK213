Never heard of Oral Health Foundation or National Smile Month?

The Oral Health Foundation is an independent charity (not for profit), based in the United Kingdoms, dedicated to improving oral health and wellbeing around the world. For more than 45 years they have continued to provide expert, independent and impartial advice on all aspects of oral health directly to those who need it most.

Formed in 1971, the concept of preventive dentistry and the benefits which could ensue from it were then very new and being practiced by only a small proportion of the dental profession. The founding fathers of the Oral Health Foundation wished to see the preventive message that 'Teeth are for Life' spread as widely as possible, with the aim of improving the overall oral health of everyone

National Smile Month is a charity campaign that focuses on spreading awareness about the benefits of good oral health and promoting the value of a healthy smile. Every year, between 13 May and 13 June, the Oral Health Foundation promotes the understanding of important healh issues and provides oral health support to some of the most vulnerable members of society.

During National Smile Month, the Oral Health Foundation aims to promote these 4 key messsages for better oral health:

  • Brush teeth for two minutes, last thing at night and one other time during the day, with a fluoride toothpaste.
  • Clean between your teeth with interdental brushes or floss, and use mouthwash every day.
  • Cut down how much and how often you have sugary foods and drinks.
  • Visit a dentist regularly.

How has Oral B supported the Oral Health Foundation?

Oral B has consistently donated to the Oral Health Foundation for more than a decade and have been involved with the National Smile Month initiative since they became the initiative’s very first Platinum sponsor.

Oral B’s continued dedication to the National Smile Month initiative allowed the charity to reach even more people with positive messages regarding oral health and hygiene as well as provide support to more groups where oral disease is far too common.

Oral B and the Oral Health Foundation’s shared vision and values, their commitment to prevention as well as sheer determination in raising awareness of the correlations between oral health and general health have led to this longstanding partnership, the overall improvement of the United Kingdom’s national health as well as the oral health of many individuals across the world.
