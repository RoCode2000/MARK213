Marketing Mix

In Singapore's competitive oral care scene, Oral-B's interdental brushes face challenges. While widely available, their limited product range lacks the diverse sizes, features, and materials seen in competitor offerings. Additionally, higher prices might deter budget-conscious consumers. While some promotions exist, a cohesive marketing strategy is missing.

Product

A small brush designed for cleaning between teeth with soft bristles and an easy-grip handle.

Learn More

Description:

  • The Oral-B interdental Brush is a small brush designed to clean between teeth.
  • It’s made of soft bristles which are gentle to gums, with an easy-grip handle that’s comfortable to use.
  • The interdental brush is available in a variety of sizes that can be used by people with different teeth gaps.

    • Benefits: The interdental brush can be used to food debris and plaques in teeth where your normal brush can reach. It prevents gingivitis and gum diseases.

    Instructions:
    1. Choose the correct brush size that fits the space between teeth.
    2. Insert the brush between the teeth and move the brush back and forth in a swaying motion.
    3. Rinse your mouth thoroughly with water.

    Product Variation: Oral-B interdental Brush offers a wide range of (ISO 00-8) to fit various teeth gaps, ensuring a comfortable and effective teeth cleaning. Oral-B interdental brush as a few different bristle options. Some include soft, medium and even ultra soft bristle for sensitive gums.

    Packaging: The product uses premium design packaging which has sleek visuals and prioritizes key features like dentist recommendation. So variations have portable cases, catering to a busy lifestyle. The packaging has clear ISO number and color coding to indicate brush sizes so that it will be easier for consumers to select.

    Innovation:

  • Oral-B interdental Clean Max: The brush indicates bristles that fade overtime. This will prompt the user to get a replacement brush for efficient cleaning.
  • Oral-B Interdental Expert Clean: The brush has a flexible neck which gives better access for hard to reach places.

    • Strength:

  • Established brand: Oral-B's brand recognition instills confidence and familiarity among existing customers.

    • Weakness:

  • Limited Sizing: The lack of size and feature options constrains the appeal to individuals with particular needs or preferences.
  • Advancement shortfall: Competitor offerings may showcase cutting-edge designs, materials, and functionalities, potentially rendering Oral-B's options outdated by comparison.

    • Price

    Employs a Price Skimming Strategy, leveraging brand recognition and dentist recommendations for a premium pricing model.

    Learn More

    Strategy: Oral-B is 17.5% more than tepe while 44% more than pearlie white. Oral-B’s high price maximizes profit to the company. At the same time being a high price product attracts competitors products.

    Pricing Tactics: Prestige Pricing
    Oral-B use their high quality image to set a higher price for their product. Oral-B benefits from brand recognition and dentist-recommended image, justifying the premium price compared to Tepe and Pearlie White.

    Market Positioning: Oral-B targets health conscious customers that prioritize quality. While Tepe and Pearlie White target a wider audience who are budget-conscious with a lower price point.

    Strength:

  • Exclusive placement: Higher prices might imply higher quality and brand value, attracting consumers seeking premium oral care solutions.
  • Profit margin: Higher prices allow for better profit margins, especially considering the relatively low production cost of interdental brushes.
  • Competitive disadvantage: Oral-B's options may become less appealing in comparison to competitor offerings with comparable features offered at lower prices.

    • Weakness:

  • Price inconsistency: Prices can vary significantly across different retailers and online platforms, potentially confusing consumers.

    • Marketing Concept:

  • Value-based pricing: Set prices based on perceived value rather than simply production cost, effectively communicating the unique benefits of Oral-B interdental brushes.
  • Price discrimination: Offer segmented pricing strategies based on customer demographics, purchase history, or behavior to reach different price sensitivity levels.

  • Dental Clinics: Oral-B partners with dental clinics to offer interdental brushes as part of oral hygiene regimens and professional recommendations. This leverages dentist expertise and builds trust among consumers.
